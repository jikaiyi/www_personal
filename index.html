About

I am an Assistant Professor in Computer Science and Engineering at the University at Buffalo. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, and a Visiting Student Research Collaborator at Princeton University.

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from The Ohio State University, advised by Prof. Yingbin Liang, and my B.E. from the University of Science and Technology of China.

Research

Foundations for efficient, adaptive learning

My group connects optimization theory with modern machine-learning practice.

01

Optimization for modern ML

Parameter-efficient optimization, multi-objective learning, and bilevel optimization.

02

Foundations of AI & ML

Multi-task learning, continual learning, and meta-learning with rigorous guarantees.

03

Foundation-model adaptation

PEFT and LoRA for multi-task and federated settings, continual tuning, and multi-objective alignment.

04

Real-world validation

Methods evaluated in robotics, recommendation systems, natural language processing, and health.
