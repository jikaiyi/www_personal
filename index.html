Bio

Research focus: theoretical and algorithmic foundations for optimization and machine learning, with applications to efficient and adaptive AI.

I am an Assistant Professor in Computer Science and Engineering at the University at Buffalo. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, and a Visiting Student Research Collaborator in Electrical Engineering at Princeton University.

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from The Ohio State University, supervised by Prof. Yingbin Liang, and my B.E. from the University of Science and Technology of China.

Selected honors: NSF CAREER Award (2025), CSE Excellence in Research Award (2025), SEAS Early Career Researcher of the Year Award (2026), and UB Exceptional Scholar Award – Young Investigator (2026).

Research overview
Optimization for modern ML

Parameter-efficient optimization, multi-objective learning, and bilevel optimization.

Foundations of AI and ML

Multi-task learning, continual learning, and meta-learning with rigorous guarantees.

Foundation-model adaptation

PEFT and LoRA for multi-task and federated settings, continual tuning, and multi-objective alignment.

Applications

Methods validated in robotics, recommendation systems, natural language processing, and health.
